Before and After

  • Tags: health,
  • Written on: July 31, 2013


  • Before

  • After

New years eve 2011 was spent celebrating with friends. It was only a few minutes before midnight and the start of 2012. I was looking foward to watching the fireworks and having another corona. Earlier in the night I asked around my friends their new years resolutions and as in years past they revolved around better health, saving money, improving employment situations all the lazy dreams of men in late twenties.

As someone who has struggled with a growing wastline all my life I have made and failed many resolutions. Making matters worse the pasing year had been particlary bad It was a perfect storm, working two jobs, overtime and long travel. I snacked and binged on takeout regularly by newyears I was at my worst.

From my friends front porch we all could see the tips of harbour bridge, my watch clicked onto midnight and soon the fireworks started 2012 had arrived it was time to get serious. Where to start? My first thought I need to get off sugar my white powder of choice, the sweet goodness that would light up my day. I would need to break the grip sugar had my life, my mood, my wasteline.

While watching the glow off the harbour bridge I pondred on a course of action and one though came soft drinks a bottle with lunch a can with dinner it was too much but cold turkey; I could not see myself following through a compromise I would step down buying smaller sizes, going a day a two a week without and eventually I could make quit for good.
